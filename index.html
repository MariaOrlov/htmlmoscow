what to do in Moscow with kids

Moscow truly is a mega-metropolis like no other. Its non-stop energy.
You must have asked yourself at some time, “Is it worth traveling to Moscow with children? Of course it is!
My name is Maria Orlov. Before I moved to Israel for 1 year I lived in Moscow and fall in love with this city. In Israel I worked as a tour-operator and created tours to Russia. All these tours were for retieries, but I do beleive that you will find what to do with children of different ages while your family trip.
You can take different excursions on any language, including hebrew, to the Kremlin, Red Square or St. Basil’s Cathedral, walks through Moscow’s public parks, or a boat cruise, which will be fascinating to both children and adults. On this website you will find places that your children will love. I classified them by themes: zoos, museums, circuses, theaters, etc. Of course it will be impossible for you to visit them all, so choose those that best suit your children’s tastes.

Fun Things for Kids

    Zoos and Aquariums
  • Moscow Zoo is one of the oldest in the world. At present it has more than 5,000 animals and about 1,000 species of fauna. It occupies 21.5 hectares.
  • Oceanarium is home to 3,000 species of fish and other animals from all around the world from South America to Lake Baikal, of all colors, shapes and sizes, such as fascinating sharks and original stingrays.
  • Moskvarium is the Center for Oceanography and Marine Biology. n the Moskvarium aquarium you can see different shows with dolphins, whales and amazing water shows, as well as a host of marine species.
    Astronomy Museums
  • Planetarium is a museum for discovering the wonderful world of stars and the progress of science, the history of astronomy and the conquest of cosmic space.
  • Museum of Cosmonautics - the museum displays space suits, space capsules, space age posters, etc.
    Science Museums
  • Experimentarium is a museum dedicated to modern science and based on the premise that science is grounded in experiments, which is why the visit to Experimentanium is a unique experience, with more than 300 screens or interactive exhibits and a host of educational exhibits for the young and not so young.
  • Museum of Human Living Systems s a unique place where you can literally connect to the most complex objects in the world – to the structure of the living systems. 130 interactive exhibit items help to understand how the living things around us work. A visitor here becomes the main object of study.
  • Orlov Museum of Paleontology - contains 5,500 natural paleontological objects and exhibits that represent almost all types of fossil organisms, such as dinosaurs from Mongolia, therapsids from the Perm (region in Russia) and Precambrian fossils from Siberia, to name a few important examples. In its six rooms you can view the main stages of the evolution of life on Earth.
  • Darvin Museum It contains many stuffed animals. There are explanations of the theory of evolution and biodiversity. In addition, they show films and have their own exhibitions.
    Circuses
  • Great Moscow State Circus is an experience that will excite the whole family. It was founded in 1971 and can accommodate 3,400 people.
  • Nikulin Circus also puts on a great show with the classic clowns, acrobats, tightrope walkers or animal tamers, all acclaimed.It is one of the oldest in the country and can accommodate more than 2,000 people.
    Theaters for children
  • Obraztsov State Puppet Theater is without doubt the one that’s most famous. It is a unique place that also has its own museum.
    Shopping in Moscow with Children
  • Central Store on Lubyanka is a seven-storey shopping mall and entertainment center for children with family activities. It is located in a magnificent building, inside and out, dating back to 1953
  • Izmailovo Kremlin apart from enjoying its wooden buildings, it is very colourful and there are many things to do, of course, with the kids. It is a very unique cultural and entertainment center.
    Parks
  • VDNKh - At almost 600 acres, this Soviet park has everything; from space museums to soviet shopping outlets to equestrian centers to beaches. In fact, there’s so much to do that there really is no way to cover it all in one day.
  • Zaradye Park - Moscow’s brand-new park couldn’t be more different from the stern, red brick of the Kremlin that towers over it. Whichever way you look, you’ll be rubbing your eyes to make sure that this other-worldly landscape of floating glass bridges, underground ancient walkways, Siberian tundra and ice caves really does exist.
    Rope Parks
  • Sky Park - is the biggest one in Russia. the high is up to 15 meters.
  • Panda Park - there are different kind of routes. suitible for kids from 3 yers old.
  • Gammy Park - there is a 1 rout on the height of 1 meter, which is suittible for children from 90 to 130 cm, and 2 others for children from 130 cm.
